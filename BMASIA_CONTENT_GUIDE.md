# BMAsia Content & Messaging Guide

## Brand Positioning

**BMAsia** is Bright Ears' background music curation service for Thai businesses. This guide helps marketing and content teams maintain consistent messaging.

---

## Key Messages

### What BMAsia Is
- Professional background music curation service
- Expert-curated playlists tailored to business type and brand identity
- Subscription-based service for ongoing music management
- Commercial music licensing compliance included

### What BMAsia Is NOT
- Live DJ bookings or event entertainment (that's main Bright Ears platform)
- One-time playlist creation service

**Solution**: Curated playlists for lobby, restaurant, spa, and pool areas that enhance brand identity

**Pain Point**: Poor music choices reduce productivity and employee satisfaction
**Solution**: Focus-friendly music for workspaces and energizing music for common areas

**Pain Point**: Low-quality meditation music breaks relaxation immersion
**Solution**: Professionally curated ambient and meditation music for treatments

### Gyms & Fitness
**Pain Point**: Mis-timed music fails to motivate members during workouts
**Solution**: High-energy music perfectly synced to cardio, weights, and class schedules

## FAQs - Approved Answers

### "How is this different from Spotify?"
**Key Points**:
- Spotify playlists are generic, ours are custom-tailored
- Commercial licensing included (Spotify personal accounts are illegal for business use)
- Ongoing management vs one-time playlist
- Expert curation vs algorithm

### "How does licensing work?"
**Key Points**:
- All music fully licensed for commercial use in Thailand
- We handle all PRO (Performance Rights Organization) compliance
- No risk of fines or legal issues
- Standard streaming services (personal Spotify, etc.) are NOT licensed for commercial use

### "Can we request specific music?"
**Key Points**:
- Yes! We encourage sharing preferences
- We'll incorporate your favorites while ensuring variety
- Can also specify artists/genres to avoid

## Tone of Voice

### English Content
- **Professional but approachable**: Business-to-business language without being stuffy
- **Benefits-focused**: Lead with what customer gains, not features
- **Confidence without arrogance**: "We're experts" not "Only we can do this"
- **Clear and concise**: No marketing fluff or jargon

### Thai Content
- **Respectful formal Thai**: Use ครับ/ค่ะ appropriately
- **Business-appropriate**: เรา (we) not ผม/ดิฉัน (I)
- **Clear benefits**: Thai businesses value direct communication
- **Cultural sensitivity**: Reference Thai holidays, cultural events when relevant

## Social Proof Guidelines

**Approved Testimonial Format**:
"[Quote about specific benefit]" - [Business Type], [City]

**Example**:
"Our hotel lobby music creates the perfect first impression for guests" - Luxury Hotel, Bangkok

**DO**:
- Use specific benefits (increased sales, longer dwell time, better atmosphere)
- Include business type and general location
- Get written permission before using testimonials

**DON'T**:
- Use fake testimonials or made-up quotes
- Include specific business names without permission
- Make unverifiable claims ("Sales increased 50%!" without proof)

## Content Update Process

### To Update Pricing
1. Update in `messages/en.json` and `messages/th.json`
2. Update in `BMASIA_PRICING_STRATEGY.md`
3. Announce changes to existing customers 30 days in advance
4. Grandfather existing customers at old pricing (optional)

### To Add New Industry
1. Add to API validation enum
2. Add translation keys
3. Update Industries component
4. Create industry-specific FAQ if needed

### To Modify FAQ
1. Update in `messages/en.json` and `messages/th.json`
2. Ensure answer accuracy (especially licensing information)
3. Keep answers under 100 words for readability

## Legal & Compliance

### Must Include
- Commercial licensing is handled by BMAsia
- Music is legal for business use in Thailand
- No hidden fees or commission

### Never Claim
- "Only legal option" (other services exist)
- Specific ROI without data ("guaranteed 50% sales increase")
- Comparison to specific competitors by name (defamation risk)
